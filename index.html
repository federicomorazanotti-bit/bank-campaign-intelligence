Pre-Call Intelligence
Three signals available before any call is made
Call duration predicts conversion - but only after the call. The three findings below are different: they are knowable from the CRM before dialling. Used together, they nearly double the campaign's baseline conversion rate on a filtered pool of calls.

The key split: Duration is the strongest predictor overall - but it is only known after the call ends. Everything below it on this chart is a pre-call signal available in any CRM before dialling. Previous Campaign Outcome vs Conversion Rate (2008-2010)
Clients who subscribed in a prior campaign convert at 64.7% - 7x the rate of unclassified contacts
Data period: 2008-2010 | n=45,211 calls | Source: Moro et al. (2014), Decision Support Systems
The most underused signal: Only 1,511 calls (3.3% of the campaign) targeted clients with a previous successful outcome - yet they converted at 64.7%. The 81.7% of calls with unknown prior outcome converted at just 9.2%. Prioritising known previous subscribers is the single highest-ROI action available from existing data.
Number of Contacts in This Campaign vs Conversion Rate (2008-2010)
Conversion rate drops sharply after the first contact - repeated calling is costly and largely ineffective
Data period: 2008-2010 | n=45,211 calls | Source: Moro et al. (2014), Decision Support Systems
Diminishing returns: First contact converts at 14.6%. By the fourth contact or beyond, the rate falls to 7.4% - half the initial rate. Yet 9,641 calls (21.3% of the entire campaign) were made to prospects already contacted 4 or more times. Stopping at 2-3 attempts and reallocating that budget to fresh high-propensity leads would materially lift overall results.
Housing Loan Status vs Conversion Rate (2008-2010)
No housing loan = 2.2x more likely to convert - and it's in the CRM before the call
Data period: 2008-2010 | n=45,211 calls | Source: Moro et al. (2014), Decision Support Systems
Free pre-call filter: Clients without a housing loan converted at 16.7% vs 7.7% for those with one - a 2.2x multiplier. Unlike call duration, this data exists before the conversation starts. It requires no model: just a CRM filter.

This is not a model prediction - it is a direct calculation from the raw dataset. Applying these three filters to the 45,211 calls in the original campaign produces a pool of 6,666 calls that achieved a 21.9% conversion rate. No machine learning, no scoring system, no additional budget required. Just smarter use of data that already existed in the CRM at the time of each call.

The implication is significant: the bottleneck in this campaign was not reach or budget - it was targeting logic. A fraction of the calls, better selected, outperforms the full campaign by nearly double on rate.

* Revenue figures use a conservative €150 margin per term deposit as a directional illustration. Actual margin will vary by institution and product terms. The per-call and uplift figures scale linearly with the assumed margin.

About This Analysis
This dashboard analyses 45,211 outbound bank telemarketing calls made by a Portuguese banking institution between May 2008 and November 2010. The goal of each call was to sell a term deposit product. All figures on the Overview, Segments, Campaigns and Targeting Intelligence tabs are computed directly from bank-full.csv, the full version of the UCI Bank Marketing Dataset.

The 2024 Context tab benchmarks each historical finding against published 2024 data from the ABA, McKinsey, and BMO. Every number in this dashboard is either derived directly from the UCI dataset or sourced from a named publication. No figures are estimated.

A Note on Monthly Aggregation
Why Jan-Dec instead of a continuous timeline
The dataset's month field records calendar month only, with no year column. Monthly charts therefore aggregate all calls from the same calendar month across the full campaign period (2008-2010). For example, the January bar represents all calls made in January across all three years combined. This is consistent with how the data is structured and appropriate for identifying seasonal patterns, but it does not represent a continuous monthly timeline. Quarterly figures on the Campaigns tab follow the same aggregation logic.
Limitations
Honest caveats every analyst should state upfront
1.
The UCI dataset covers one Portuguese banking institution only. Results may not generalise to other institutions, geographies or time periods.
2.
The dataset records outbound telemarketing call outcomes only. It does not capture digital, branch or inbound sales from the same period.
3.
Call duration is recorded after the fact - it cannot be used as a pre-call predictor. It is an outcome signal, not an input variable.
4.
The targeting scenario (21.9% rate) is a direct computation from the raw data, not a model prediction. Real-world application would require CRM data matching and would depend on list quality.
5.
The 2024 benchmarks reflect US and global banking trends and may not apply to all markets or product types.
6.
Conversion rate comparisons between the UCI data and 2024 benchmarks are directional only - the product types, markets and methodologies differ.
